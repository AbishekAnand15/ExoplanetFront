FAQ

Who is ExoDetect useful for?

ExoDetect is useful for students, astronomy enthusiasts, and early-stage researchers interested in learning how exoplanet transit signals are detected from real observational data.

What is a TIC ID?

A TIC (TESS Input Catalog) ID uniquely identifies a star observed by NASAâ€™s TESS mission and is used to retrieve its light curve data.

What kind of data does ExoDetect analyze?

The tool analyzes publicly available photometric light curves from NASAâ€™s Transiting Exoplanet Survey Satellite (TESS), accessed through the MAST archive.

Does this confirm the existence of exoplanets?

No. ExoDetect identifies potential transit-like signals. All detections are candidates and require follow-up observations and validation by professional pipelines.

What analysis methods are used?

The pipeline uses light curve preprocessing, noise reduction, transit folding, depth estimation, and signal-to-noise evaluation to assess potential transit events.

Why does the analysis sometimes take time?

Each request downloads real astronomical data and performs multiple computational steps, which may take several seconds depending on data size and server load.

What are the limitations of this tool?

ExoDetect does not perform full astrophysical validation, stellar false-positive rejection, or multi-sector analysis. It is intended for educational and exploratory purposes.

Is ExoDetect an official NASA tool?

No. This is an independent student research project and is not affiliated with or endorsed by NASA.

Can this be used for learning or research?

Yes. ExoDetect is well-suited for learning signal processing, exoplanet detection concepts, and working with real astronomical data.
